About me:

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in the Mechanical Engineering department at UC Berkeley. My research interests lie in Theoretical and Applied Fluid Dynamics, Dynamical Systems, Geophysical Fluid Dynamics.

I am being advised by Prof. M. Reza Alam and a part of TAF-Lab group at Berkeley.

I graduated from Sharif University of Technology undergraduate program in 2013. During my undergrad studies, I worked with Prof. Mir A. Jalali on Simulation of Perturbed Stellar Systems Using CUDA programming. I also worked with Prof. M. Reza Ejtehadi on Aggregation Properties of Stiff Polyelectrolytes in Presence of Multivalent Salt.

My research is funded in part by National Science Foundation. I am the PI for Berkeley Fluids Seminar since 2015.
