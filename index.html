About me:

I am a postdoctoral fellow in Applied Mathematics in the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at the Harvard University.

I got my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering department at UC Berkeley. I was advised by Prof. M. Reza Alam in Theoretical and Applied Fluids group at UC Berkeley, and I worked on nonlinear waves. My research was funded in part by National Science Foundation and I was the PI for Berkeley Fluids Seminar from 2015 - 2018. I got my M.A. in Mathematics at UC Berkeley under supervision of Prof. Jon Wilkening, where I worked on Quantum Hydrodynamic Analogs: Euler-Schrödinger Transformation. Prior to my graduate studies, I got my B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering and in Physics from Sharif University of Technology in 2013. During my undergrad studies, I worked with Prof. Mir A. Jalali on Simulation of Perturbed Stellar Systems Using CUDA programming.
